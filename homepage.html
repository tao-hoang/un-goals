Mental Health Consequences of War

Get Help


Immediate Mental Health Helpline

More than one-in-five people living in conflict-affected areas suffers from a mental illness, according to a new report based on UN figures, prompting the World Health Organization (WHO) to call for increased, sustained investment in mental health services in those zones. Mental health is a huge concern around the world to be taken care of.


Peace Around the World

It's not the only people in the war zone whose mental health is affected, but breaking news and images about war, death, and destruction floods the media, it can be overwhelming and scary. For some, this may affect emotional health, triggering anxiety and stress.
